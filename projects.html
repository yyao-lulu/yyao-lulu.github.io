Projects

1. Why is a TED talk popular?

This is a final project of the course Data Science I. Here we utilized R to statistically analyze the 2550 TED talks from June 2006 to Sept. 2017 that are hosted on official TED website. We primarily based our analysis on the topic and speakers. Then according to the comment words selected by the audience, we conducted a sentiment analysis to show the relationship between views and sentiment score. Finally, we built a linear model taking views as response and several variables as predictors. The model fits the data quite well and can be used for future prediction. For more details, please visit the project website.

2. Instacart dashboards with Plotly and Shiny

Instacart is an online grocery service that allows you to shop online from local stores. In New York City, partner stores include Whole Foods, Fairway, and The Food Emporium. Instacart offers same-day delivery, and items that users purchase are delivered within 2 hours. We conducted graphical analysis with Plotly and Shiny on the shopping dataset and embeded it into flexdashboards. The project website is here.
